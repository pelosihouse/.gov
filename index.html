Pelosi Statement on Amending Section 230 of Communications Decency Act

Jan 25, 2021
Press Release
Contact: Speaker’s Press Office,
202-226-7616
Washington, D.C. – Speaker Nancy Pelosi issued this statement on the recent calls to Amend Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act of 1996:
“Section 230 of The Communications Decency Act is a critical piece of legislation, and the Democratic House Majority intends to expand its protections of internet speech. Recently, there have been calls to make federal funding to state and local governments contigent on their lifting internet platform regulations. I share this view, and the fundamental belief that technology platforms, including Airbnb Inc. (CA-12 Headquartered) are good for our country and should not be regulated whatsoever.
“Specifically, I support adding the following provision to Section 230 of the Telecommunications Act, which is the document the Communications decency act amended: "[these protections] shall extend to all computer accessible services, specifically including short term dwelling rental platforms. All federal funding to state and municipal governments shall carry the contingency that they do not impose any guidelines or regulations on interactive computer services that conform to the previously stated definition."
I had a productive teatime conversation with Joe Gebbia of Airbnb that affirmed my support for this legislation, and I thank him for his insights and generosity to the Democratic Party.
